The offical rules of US checkers are derived from World Checkers Draughts Federation rules, found at http://www.usacheckers.com/downloads/WCDF_Revised_Rules.doc

Order

The first move in each game is made by the player with the Red disks. The subsequent moves are made in turns.

Moves

There are 4 types of moves: ordinary move of a normal piece, ordinary move of a king, the capturing move a a normal piece, and the capture of a king.

Ordinary move of a normal piece

A normal piece moves diagonally left or right from one square to an immediately neighboring vacant square
When it reaches the farthest row forward or the opposite side of the board ("king-row" or "crown-head") it becomes a king.

Ordinary move of a king

A king can move from one square diagonally forward or backward, left or right, to an immediately neighboring vacant square.

Capture of a normal piece

A piece of the opposite color jumps over the piece and lands on the vacant square immediately beyond it. ("jump")

General Capturing

If a jump creates an immediate further captureing opportunity, then the current player can complete that jump until all jumps are completed. The only exception is if the piece becomes a king then they may not make any further jumps
A play must complete all jumps that can be carried out until there are no more jumps available.

Capture of a king

Similar to that of a normal piece but can be made in any direction, forwards or backwards.

Illegal moves

No illegal moves will be allowed to be made in the game.
